About Galette des Rois

A January Tradition

Galette des Rois (King Cake) is a French pastry tradition celebrated every January for Epiphany. A golden puff pastry filled with rich almond frangipane, it hides a small figurine called a feve. Whoever finds the feve in their slice is crowned king or queen for the day.

Why Japan?

Tokyo and other Japanese cities have become one of the best places outside France to find high-quality galettes. French-trained pastry chefs and luxury hotel patisseries create exceptional versions each January, but finding them requires knowing where to look.
